What is volumetric weight?

Volumetric weight is the calculated weight based on the length, width and height of a parcel used to determine its dimensional weight. The formula used to calculate the volumetric weight varies by carrier and service type.

Why is volumetric weight charged?

The larger a parcel is, the more space it takes up in a vehicle or aircraft, and therefore the more it costs to transport. Large but lightweight parcels can take up space that could be used for heavier items, so carriers use volumetric weight to ensure fair pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is volumetric weight?
Volumetric weight is a pricing technique used by carriers to account for lightweight but bulky packages.
Why is volumetric weight used?
It ensures carriers can charge appropriately for packages that take up valuable space but don't weigh much.
How is it calculated for courier services?
Most couriers use (L × W × H) / 5000 (where dimensions are in cm) to calculate volumetric weight.
How is it calculated for freight?
Freight services often use (L × W × H) / 6000 or other divisors depending on the carrier.
